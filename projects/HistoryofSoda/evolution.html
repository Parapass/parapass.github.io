    Evolution

    After Coca-Cola was invented, many people started opening up shops to sell copycat formulas due to the sheer popularity of it. One of these competitiors is Pepsi-Cola.

    Pepsi-Cola was invented by a pharmascist named Caleb Bradham, who was hoping to recreate the success of Coca-Cola. The drink instantly became a hit, in which Bradham soon founded Pepsi-Cola.

    After many years of being the second best choice to Coca-Cola, hard times fell on the Pepsi-Cola when World War I came along and bumped up sugar prices.

    Bradham soon took a big gamble, and decided to buy a lot of sugar with all of his money because he feared the prices would go even higher.

    Unfortunatly, sugar prices fell from $0.22 per bag to $0.02 per bag soon after he bought it. Bradham had to give away the company to investor Roy Megargel after going bankrupt.

    Now in complete control, Megargel gave his own company a loan and moved offices to Richmond, Virginia, however his reign over Pepsi-Cola would not last long due to the Great Depression.

    When it went bankrupt, Roy Megargel telephoned Charles Guth, the current owner of Loft, Inc. that sold chocolate candy and soft drinks. They formed a new corporation, the Pepsi-Cola Company.

    Loft's quickly replaced all of their soda fountains wih Pepsi-Cola, which led to lower sales, but Guth didn't want to give up on the company.
